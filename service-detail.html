Embroidery & Handwork Services

Our skilled artisans bring garments to life with detailed hand embroidery, traditional motifs, and intricate finishing techniques that celebrate centuries of craftsmanship.

About Our Embroidery Services

At GrowFashion, we preserve the art of traditional embroidery by combining age-old techniques with contemporary designs. Each piece is meticulously crafted by our master artisans who have honed their skills over decades.

Our embroidery services transform ordinary garments into extraordinary works of art, perfect for weddings, festivals, and special occasions where tradition meets modern elegance.

Techniques We Specialize In

  • Zardozi Embroidery: Gold and silver thread work with precious stones and pearls
  • Kashmiri Crewel: Wool embroidery on fabric with intricate floral patterns
  • Chikankari: Delicate white thread embroidery on fine muslin and cotton
  • Phulkari: Vibrant floral embroidery from Punjab using silk threads
  • Kantha Stitch: Running stitch embroidery from West Bengal with narrative motifs
  • Mirror Work: Traditional embroidery incorporating small mirrors for reflective beauty
  • Sequins & Beadwork: Intricate embellishment with sequins, beads, and crystals

The Embroidery Process

Every embroidered garment goes through a meticulous process:

1. Design Consultation: We work with you to create or select patterns that reflect your personal style and the occasion.

2. Fabric Selection: Choosing the right base fabric that complements the embroidery technique.

3. Pattern Tracing: Master artisans trace the design onto the fabric using traditional methods.

4. Hand Embroidery: Each stitch is carefully placed by skilled hands, ensuring precision and beauty.

5. Quality Check: Multiple quality inspections throughout the process to ensure perfection.

6. Finishing: Final touches including cleaning, pressing, and packaging.

Customization Options

We offer complete customization for your embroidery needs:

  • Personalized motifs and monograms
  • Color matching to your specific requirements
  • Mix of traditional and contemporary patterns
  • Choice of thread types (silk, cotton, metallic, wool)
  • Various density options from minimal to fully covered embroidery
